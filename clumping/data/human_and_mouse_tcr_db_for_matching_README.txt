These mouse and human databases of single and paired TCR sequences combines data from the VDJdb and other sources, as indicated in the 'db' and 'dataset' columns. The citations for the values in the db column are given below. Duplicate receptors have been removed. In the case of duplicate receptors, the priority ordering of the databases is as follows: ['vdjdb', 'thomas', '10x_200k', 'pdb', 'mcpas', 'tet_tcr_seq', 'FHCRC_unpublished'].

V., Van Dyk, E., Dash, P., Attaf, M., Rius, C., Ladell, K., McLaren, J. E., Matthews, K. K., Clemens, E. B., … Chudakov, D. M. (2018). VDJdb: a curated database of T-cell receptor sequences with known antigen specificity. Nucleic Acids Research, 46(D1), D419–D427.

10x_200k: 10x_Genomics. (2020). A new way of exploring immunity: linking highly multiplexed antigen recognition to immune repertoire and phenotype (Application Note LIT000047 Rev C ). Retrieved from the 10X Genomics website: https://pages.10xgenomics.com/rs/446-PBO-704/images/10x_AN047_IP_A_New_Way_of_Exploring_Immunity_Digital.pdf

mcpas: Tickotsky, N., Sagiv, T., Prilusky, J., Shifrut, E., & Friedman, N. (2017). McPAS-TCR: a manually curated catalogue of pathology-associated T cell receptor sequences. Bioinformatics , 33(18), 2924–2929.

pdb: Berman, H. M., Westbrook, J., Feng, Z., Gilliland, G., Bhat, T. N., Weissig, H., Shindyalov, I. N., & Bourne, P. E. (2000). The protein data bank. Nucleic Acids Research, 28(1), 235–242.
